Movie Review


Premalu


Rating : 7.8/10★ ★ ★☆

Review

Premalu is a breezy, youthful rom-com that finds charm in simplicity. Directed by Girish A. D., the film tracks the love story of Sachin and Reenu — two young souls navigating adulthood, jobs, and the awkward, hopeful energy of first relationships. The movie doesn’t try for deep complexity; instead it leans on relatable humour, lighthearted drama, and the everyday-life struggles and dreams of fresh graduates. The performances — especially by Naslen and Mamitha Baiju — feel natural and easy, and the supporting cast brings in enough fun and quirk to keep things entertaining.

What makes Premalu stand out is how it celebrates the charm of ordinariness — the cityscape, the small-time jobs, the uncertainties of youth — without making them melodramatic. The music by Vishnu Vijay adds to the easygoing vibe, and the film balances humour and sentiment in a way that feels honest rather than forced. While the story may not be groundbreaking or radically different from standard rom-com tropes, the film’s warmth, youthful energy, and relatability make it a comforting, feel-good watch. For those who enjoy down-to-earth tales of young love and life’s small dramas, Premalu delivers just the right kind of simplicity.
